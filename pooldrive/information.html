What is Carbon Footprint ?

Most of our day-to-day activities leaves behind a carbon footprint. A carbon foot print is the estimated amount of green house gases produced by our day to day activities over a period of time. Carbon Dioxide is the gas, mainly emitted by our activities. Hence our Carbon footprint is measured by the amount of carbon dioxide emitted by us. Other gases are converted to Carbon Equivalent.




Greenhouse Gases

Greenhouse gases are gases that can trap the sun's rays. This way they retain the temperature of the Earth These gases are known as Greenhouse gases. Carbon dioxide, Ozone, Methane, Water Vapour etc. are examples for greenhouse gases. Even though they occur naturally an increase in their amount in the atmosphere cause the phenomenon known as Global Warming.




How do you make Carbon Footprint ?

Many of our daily activities such as burning fuels,solid materials, diposing wastes etc. emit greenhouse gases. Industrial activities and use of chemical fertilizers also emit greenhouse gases, mainly nitrous oxide. When leftover food items, grass, vegetables etc.. are left to decompose they release Methane. If this released Methane is not efficiently used using bio compost or other means it would get deposited in the atmosphere increasing the risk of global warming. Chemical fertilizers also release this gas.
